# 4Wind Lane3 Cut Path 2026-03-22

## Purpose

- Record how the `lane3_class` cut was reached while refining `4wind`.
- This is a discovery path note, not a final adoption memo.

## Starting Point

The path started from the odds-based re-run of `4wind` over `2025-04-01..2026-03-20`.

The useful intermediate shape was:

- `only_wind_5_6`
- partners narrowed from `4-1 / 4-5 / 4-6` to `4-1 / 4-5`
- then a market-side filter: `min quoted odds 10-50`

At that point, the working slice was:

- condition: `wind 5-6m`, structural `4wind`, bet `4-1 / 4-5`, `min_odds 10-50`
- races: `395-396`
- ROI: about `173%`

## Why Look At Lane 2 / Lane 3

After the market filter, the next question was not only "when lane 4 is strong" but also:

- what kind of inner race shape lets `4-1 / 4-5` survive?
- are lanes `2` and `3` just noise, or are they part of the structure?

This led to slicing the same `4-1 / 4-5 + wind 5-6 + min_odds 10-50` sample by lane-2 and lane-3 racer class.

## Class Slice Result

### Lane 2 class

- `B1`: `185 races`, ROI `229.49%`
- `A1`: `105 races`, ROI `190.38%`
- `A2`: `89 races`, ROI `67.87%`
- `B2`: `17 races`, ROI `0%`

Lane 2 already showed a warning sign:

- `lane2 = A2` was clearly weak

### Lane 3 class

- `B2`: `10 races`, ROI `0%`
- `B1`: `200 races`, ROI `151.75%`
- `A2`: `95 races`, ROI `181.0%`
- `A1`: `91 races`, ROI `230.11%`

This was cleaner than lane 2:

- ROI rose in a natural order from `B2 -> B1 -> A2 -> A1`

That suggested a structural reading:

- when lane 3 is strong, the inner shape becomes more competitive
- that tension can help lane 4 attack through while still leaving `1` or `5` as the 2nd-place partner

## Working Hypothesis

The class slice led to the practical shorthand:

- `3L = A`
- meaning `lane3_class in ('A1', 'A2')`

This was treated as a cleaner first condition than trying to overfit lane-2 and lane-3 pair combinations.

## Check Without The Odds Filter

To test whether `3L = A` was only a market interaction or a structural effect, the same idea was checked again after removing the odds filter.

Comparison:

- base: `4-1 / 4-5 + wind 5-6`, races `662`, ROI `142.11%`
- plus `lane3_class in ('A1', 'A2')`, races `269`, ROI `156.41%`

Interpretation:

- the improvement survived even after the price filter was removed
- so `3L = A` looked more like a structure-side condition than a pure pricing artifact

## Current Meaning

- `3L = A` should now be read as one of the main structural components found inside refined `4wind`
- it is stronger than a mere helper cut because the improvement survived even after the odds filter was removed
- the remaining open question is not whether it matters, but whether it should be fixed into the next official refined definition
